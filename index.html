How to Use Your Ceiling Fan With Your Air Conditioner to Save Money

Many people use their ceiling fans in their home until it just gets too hot. Then they turn the fan off and rely on their Air Conditioner

That's a mistake.

Using your air conditioner and ceiling fan together can save you some serious money.

Butt to do that, you have to use them properly.

Here's how.

  1. Make sure your fan is spinning the right direction

    2. The fan needs to turn in the right direction to actually cool you off. Don't worry about clockwise or counterclockwise
    The fan should be pushing air down. You should be able to feel a breeze when standing directly under it. If you can't feel the breeze, turn off the fan and flip the switch that changes the direction of the blades

  2. Turn up the Thermostat

    3. This step is vital because if you only turn on your fan, you'll actually be using more energy than you were without it. Turn up your thermostat 2-3.
    The breeze created by your fan should keep you comfortable. According to the ,If you use air-conditioning, a ceiling fan will allow you to raise the thermostat setting about 40F with no reduction in comfort.

  3. Lastly, turn off fans in unoccupied rooms

    4. It's important to note that fans do NOT lower the temperaturein your home. They simply make the air feel cooler. This is the same reason windy days feel colder than normal days even if the temperature is the same. So leaving the fans on in rooms that have no one in them wastes money.

    So it's not really the ceiling fan...
    As you can see, the ceiling fan itself doesn't save you the money on air conditioning costs-thesavings come from turning up your thermostat.
